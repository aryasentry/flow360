# War Room Notes: PrithviGrid Monsoon Outage Response

War room owner: Harish Nambiar  
Flow360 owner: Raghav Bendre  
Safety owner: Ishita Rao  
Date: 27 Jun 2026  
Classification: Field operations war room, outage response, SLA risk

## Purpose

The war room was opened after monsoon rain increased outage and maintenance risk across Maharashtra. The team reviewed critical feeder status, technician availability, ETA communication, and safety exceptions.

## Situation Overview

PrithviGrid has eleven open outage or maintenance tickets. Four feeders are critical because they serve hospital, municipal, or industrial customers. Nashik hospital feeder HF-12 is the highest urgency item because it supports two emergency wards. Pune industrial feeder PI-07 has penalty exposure if restoration updates are delayed.

The preferred Nashik crew is delayed near Igatpuri because of rain and traffic. A Pune backup crew is available, but one technician's safety refresher status must be confirmed. Ishita Rao stated that customer SLA pressure cannot override lockout-tagout and certification requirements.

## Decisions

1. HF-12 remains critical priority until restoration is complete.
2. Dispatch recommendation must include crew certification status.
3. Customer update must be sent every two hours for hospital feeders.
4. If the preferred crew is delayed by more than 60 minutes, evaluate certified backup crew.
5. Do not promise ETA until safety approval status is known.

## Operational Signals

The outage is not only technical. It combines weather delay, asset criticality, customer communication, and safety compliance. A strong recommendation should reason across all four areas. The previous SLA breach happened because certification was checked too late; that memory should influence the dispatch decision.

## Planner Instruction

The next best action should prioritize certified technician availability and customer ETA communication. The recommendation should cite feeder criticality, monsoon delay, refresher status, and past SLA breach RCA. Human review should be required before sending ETA commitments to the customer.
