## 3. Individual Notes

### Kavya Raman

Kavya is the executive sponsor and the strongest signal for renewal confidence. She is not opposed to premium rates when they protect clinical coverage, but she strongly dislikes last-minute surprises. Her most common phrase during escalations is that "speed without readiness creates more work for the hospitals."

Flow360 should recommend executive updates when a staffing risk can affect facility coverage or renewal trust. Updates to Kavya should include the decision, owner, blocker, and next update time.

### Rohan Kulkarni

Rohan is the economic buyer and controls approval for premium-rate candidates above threshold. He prefers concise decision notes rather than operational detail. He will reject vague requests that rely only on urgency. He expects vacancy cost, overtime exposure, candidate readiness, and backup availability to be part of the approval packet.

### Meera Nair

Meera is the compliance lead and should be treated as a required reviewer for clinical candidate blockers. She responds quickly when the request contains candidate name, license ID or issuing board, start date, exact missing item, and decision needed. She is frustrated by broad messages like "please check if candidate is okay."

### Siddharth Menon

Siddharth owns the practical shortlist decision. He will accept conditional candidates only when the conditional status is explicit and paired with expected resolution date. He values backup options because ICU coverage can change quickly.
