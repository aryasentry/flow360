## 1. Meeting Objective

The objective of the call was to confirm Aarogya's urgent staffing requirements for ICU nurses, emergency ward nurses, and Epic analysts; identify approval blockers; and agree on the format of the shortlist expected by 4 PM. The meeting also addressed the customer's request for replacement coverage and commercial approval for premium-rate candidates.

## 2. Customer Opening Statement

Kavya Raman opened the meeting by stating that Aarogya cannot afford another avoidable start-date miss before the August renewal checkpoint. She said the May staffing incident created pressure from facility administrators because two planned ICU starts were delayed after credentialing blockers appeared late. Kavya emphasized that the team values speed, but not at the cost of hidden risk.

Kavya requested that Flow360 separate candidates into verified, conditional, blocked, and backup categories. She said the client team does not want to discover after selection that a candidate is not actually ready to start.

- 3 Epic analysts before 2026-07-12.

Siddharth said the first ICU shortlist is required by 4 PM because four facilities are already using overtime coverage. He said the shortlist can include conditional candidates only if the conditional status is obvious and paired with an expected clearance date.

## 5. Commercial Discussion

Rohan Kulkarni stated that Aarogya is prepared to consider premium candidates where the business case is clear. He said premium rates above 10 percent must be justified and rates above 12 percent require his explicit approval. He requested a one-page brief instead of a long resume packet.

Rohan asked that the brief include vacancy cost, overtime exposure, patient-care continuity risk, backup candidate availability, and the reason the candidate is worth approving.

Kavya asked whether Flow360 can guarantee replacement coverage if a candidate drops before day 10. Ananya Suresh said the account team can recommend a replacement guarantee for fully credentialed candidates, but not for candidates who are presented with known credentialing blockers. Meera supported that position and said the guarantee should not create a compliance promise that the team cannot support.
