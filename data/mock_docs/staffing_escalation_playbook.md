Version: 3.1
Owner: Flow360 Workforce Operations
Primary Demo Account: Aarogya Health Network
Classification: Internal operating playbook

## 1. Purpose

This playbook defines how the account team should respond when urgent staffing demand, credentialing blockers, premium-rate approvals, or customer confidence risks appear in a strategic healthcare account. It is intended to help the planner agent and human account manager choose the next best action with clear ownership and evidence.

The playbook is not a generic customer support script. It is an escalation operating model for situations where a delayed staffing decision can create SLA breach, overtime cost, patient-care continuity risk, or renewal exposure.

## 2. Strategic Account Conditions

An account is treated as strategic when one or more of the following conditions apply:

- Annual contract value exceeds INR 5 Cr.
- Renewal is within 120 days.
- Account has open executive escalation.
- Account experienced an SLA miss in the previous two quarters.
- Open roles support emergency, ICU, hospital, public infrastructure, or critical service operations.
- Client has asked for a guarantee, penalty waiver, or commercial exception.

Aarogya Health Network currently meets the strategic threshold because renewal is due on 2026-08-31, the account has prior credentialing-related SLA misses, and open ICU roles directly affect overtime coverage.

## 3. Escalation Severity Levels

| Severity | Definition | Example | Maximum Response Time |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| SEV-1 | Customer-critical staffing risk with start date inside five days or executive escalation | ICU start blocked by license verification | 2 business hours |
| SEV-2 | Material risk to SLA, renewal, or commercial approval | CFO approval required for premium candidate | Same business day |
| SEV-3 | Operational delay that can become customer-visible | Candidate missing shift consent | 1 business day |
| SEV-4 | Informational follow-up or low-risk missing context | Optional stakeholder preference | 2 business days |

## 4. Standard Escalation Workflow

1. Confirm the business event.
   - Identify what changed: new role, new deadline, blocker, complaint, or risk.
   - Link the event to source evidence such as CRM update, meeting note, email, candidate profile, policy, or incident record.

2. Classify severity.
   - Use the severity table above.
   - If uncertain, choose the higher severity until evidence reduces risk.

3. Identify decision owner.
   - Compliance blocker: Meera Nair or client compliance lead.
   - Candidate availability: assigned recruiter.
   - Rate approval: Rohan Kulkarni or account manager preparing the brief.
   - SLA breach risk: account manager plus staffing operations lead.
   - Renewal risk: executive sponsor and customer success lead.

4. Select the next best action.
   - The action must be specific, time-bound, and tied to the blocker.
   - Avoid vague actions like "check status" or "follow up."

5. Prepare evidence packet.
   - Include the minimum evidence needed for the owner to decide.
   - Do not overload the customer with raw transcripts.

6. Record memory.
   - Update Flow360 with the event, decision, owner, due date, and outcome.

## 5. Escalation Patterns And Recommended Actions

### Pattern A: Credentialing Blocker Near Start Date

Signals:

- Candidate profile says license verification, BGV, immunization, or shift consent is pending.
- Meeting notes mention start date inside five days.
- Prior incident notes mention delayed start due to late credentialing.

Recommended action:

"Escalate the exact credentialing blocker to the client compliance lead before shortlist delivery. Mark candidate as conditional or blocked until evidence clears."

Evidence to attach:

- Candidate profile
- Credentialing checklist
- Current meeting note
- Prior SLA RCA if relevant

### Pattern B: Premium Rate Requires Approval

Signals:

- Candidate rate variance is above 10 percent.
- CFO or procurement requested cost justification.
- Account is renewal-sensitive and under coverage pressure.

Recommended action:

"Prepare premium-rate approval brief comparing vacancy cost, overtime cost, coverage risk, and backup candidate availability."

Evidence to attach:

- Rate card policy
- CFO email
- Open requisition summary
- Renewal risk note

### Pattern C: Replacement Guarantee Requested

Signals:

- Customer asks for replacement guarantee.
- Candidate credentialing status is not fully verified.
- Account has prior SLA issue.

Recommended action:

"Offer replacement guarantee only for fully credentialed candidates and document exclusions for conditional candidates."

Evidence to attach:

- Replacement guarantee policy
- Credentialing checklist
- Candidate status table

### Pattern D: Renewal Confidence At Risk

Signals:

- Executive sponsor complains about surprises or missed commitments.
- Account sentiment is amber or red.
- Renewal date is within 120 days.

Recommended action:

"Create executive recovery cadence with status update, blocker owner, due date, and risk trend."

Evidence to attach:

- CRM account history
- Renewal risk note
- Recent meeting note
- SLA incident RCA

## 6. Communication Standards

Escalation messages should be short, structured, and decision-oriented. The message must include:

- What happened
- Why it matters
- What decision is needed
- Who owns the next step
- Deadline
- Evidence attached

Example:

"Meera, Ananya Sharma's BGV is complete but Karnataka license verification is still pending. Aarogya requested the ICU shortlist by 4 PM and the start date is within five days. Please confirm by 2:30 PM whether she can be shown as conditional, or whether she should be held back until verification clears."

## 7. Human Review Requirement

The agent may recommend an action, draft a message, and prepare evidence, but it must not send external communication or mark a candidate ready without human approval. Human-in-the-loop review is mandatory for:

- Candidate status changes from blocked to conditional or verified.
- Commercial exceptions.
- Replacement guarantee offers.
- Renewal-risk recovery plans.
- Any message to executive sponsor or CFO.

## 8. Success Metrics

The escalation is considered successful if:

- The blocker has a named owner and due date.
- The customer receives an update before the promised time.
- Shortlist labels are accurate.
- Premium approvals have evidence.
- No avoidable SLA miss occurs.
- Flow360 memory is updated with the decision and outcome.
