# Playbook: Enterprise SaaS Renewal Save Motion

Document owner: Customer Success Operations  
Applies to: Red-health enterprise SaaS accounts within 120 days of renewal  
Customer example: NavaPay Fintech  
Version: 2026.06  
Classification: Renewal playbook, customer success operations

## Purpose

This playbook describes the standard save motion for enterprise SaaS accounts where renewal is threatened by product reliability, adoption risk, or procurement pressure. It is designed to help Flow360 convert account memory into precise next best actions.

## Entry Criteria

Use this playbook when:

- renewal is within 120 days
- customer health is red or amber
- executive sponsor has requested a recovery plan
- procurement is comparing alternatives
- at least one purchased module has weak adoption
- open incidents remain unresolved or recently repeated

## Required Save Plan

Every save plan must include:

1. Executive sponsor and decision owner.
2. Technical owner and product owner.
3. Commercial owner if procurement is active.
4. Root cause summary for incidents.
5. 30-day recovery milestones.
6. Success metrics agreed with the customer.
7. Human-approved customer-facing commitment.

## Risk Patterns

Reliability-driven renewal risk should be handled before price defense. If Flow360 tries to negotiate pricing before proving recovery, procurement will anchor the discussion on discounting. Adoption-driven risk should be handled with workflow labs using customer examples, not generic training.

## Recommended Next Best Actions

For NavaPay-like accounts, the planner should rank actions in this order:

1. Assign named incident commander and publish 30-day reliability plan.
2. Run reconciliation workflow lab with operations lead.
3. Send executive update tying reliability metrics to renewal confidence.
4. Prepare commercial response only after operational plan is approved.

## Human Review Requirement

Any action that promises dedicated support, service credits, discounts, or custom SLAs must be reviewed by a human before execution.
