## 1. Purpose

This checklist defines the minimum evidence required before a clinical candidate may be submitted, shortlisted, marked conditional, or marked start-ready for Aarogya Health Network. It exists because prior staffing delays were caused by late discovery of license and background-verification blockers. The process is designed to prevent candidate presentation before the account team has reliable evidence of credentialing readiness.

The checklist must be used for ICU nurses, emergency ward nurses, ward coordinators, allied clinical staff, and any candidate who will access patient-care areas. Epic analysts and non-clinical technical contractors use a lighter onboarding checklist, but identity proof, background verification, confidentiality agreement, and facility access approval still apply.

## 2. Decision Rule Summary

No candidate should be represented as fully available unless all mandatory evidence items are complete and recorded. A candidate may be shown as conditional only when the hiring manager has explicitly accepted the outstanding item and the account team has given a clear expected resolution date. If the start date is within five calendar days, conditional candidates require escalation to the client compliance lead before shortlist delivery.

## 3. Required Evidence Before Shortlist Submission

| Evidence Category | Required Item | Acceptable Proof | Owner | Mandatory Before Shortlist |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Identity | PAN or passport identity proof | Clear scanned copy and recruiter verification note | Recruiter | Yes |
| Clinical license | Active state nursing/clinical license | License ID, issuing board, expiry date, verification screenshot or board email | Credentialing Associate | Yes for clinical roles |
| Background verification | Criminal/background check status | BGV vendor status or verified completion note | Compliance Operations | Yes |
| Employment history | Most recent employer check | Reference note or employment verification report | Recruiter | Yes for senior roles |

| Start-date confirmation | Candidate acceptance of proposed start date | Written candidate confirmation | Recruiter | Yes |
| Replacement eligibility | Replacement guarantee conditions | Verified credentialing and backup candidate note | Account Manager | Before offering guarantee |

## 4. Credentialing Status Definitions

Verified means every mandatory evidence item has been collected, reviewed, and recorded. The candidate can be presented as ready, subject to final client selection.

Conditional means one or more non-final evidence items are pending but the risk is known, documented, and accepted by the client hiring manager. Conditional status must be visible in the shortlist. It must not be hidden in notes.

Blocked means one or more mandatory evidence items are missing and the candidate must not be treated as fully cleared. Blocked candidates may remain in the internal pipeline but should not be counted as committed coverage.

Rejected means the candidate failed a mandatory check, declined required shift conditions, exceeded rate threshold without approval, or cannot meet the start date.

## 5. Five-Day Start Window Control

For any start date within five calendar days, the account team must run a same-day readiness review before presenting the candidate. This review must include the recruiter, compliance associate, account manager, and client compliance lead where required. The purpose is to catch blockers before the client believes a candidate is ready.

For urgent Aarogya roles, the following items must be explicitly called out in the shortlist table:

- Candidate name
- Role
- Facility or city
- Proposed start date
- License status
- BGV status
- Immunization status
- Shift consent status
- Rate variance
- Outstanding blockers
- Backup candidate availability
- Recommended label: Verified, Conditional, Blocked, or Backup

## 7. Audit Notes Required In Flow360

Every credentialing decision must leave an audit note in Flow360 memory. The note must describe what was checked, who checked it, evidence source, status, and remaining risk. A good note is specific enough for another account manager to understand why the recommendation was made without reopening raw documents.

Example audit note:

"Ananya Sharma is blocked for final shortlist as of 2026-06-27 11:40 IST. Karnataka nursing license verification is pending. BGV is complete. Start date requested by Aarogya is 2026-07-02. Meera Nair should be asked to confirm whether conditional presentation is acceptable before the 4 PM shortlist."

## 8. Prohibited Shortlist Practices

The following practices are not permitted for Aarogya:

- Presenting a candidate as ready while license verification is pending.
- Placing credentialing blockers only in a private recruiter note.
- Offering a replacement guarantee before credentialing is verified.
- Combining verified and conditional candidates in one list without labels.
- Asking the CFO for rate approval without vacancy-cost and overtime-risk context.
- Waiting until after client selection to run license verification.

## 9. Quality Review

The account manager must review two random credentialing packets each week during active hiring periods. Any missing mandatory evidence creates a corrective action. Repeated misses must be escalated to the staffing operations lead because Aarogya is a renewal-sensitive account.

## 10. Flow360 Recommendation Guidance

When Flow360 sees a pending license, BGV, immunization, or shift-consent item near a start date, it should recommend an action that names the owner, deadline, and evidence source. The recommendation should not merely say "follow up." It should state the business risk and the required decision.

Preferred recommendation structure:

- Action: Escalate specific blocker.
- Owner: Named role.
- Due: Same-day time window.
- Evidence: Candidate profile, credentialing checklist, meeting note, and prior SLA incident.
- Business metric: Reduce start-date miss risk and protect renewal confidence.
