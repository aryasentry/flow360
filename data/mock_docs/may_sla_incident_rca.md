Customer Sentiment Impact: Green to Amber

## 1. Executive Summary

Two planned ICU nurse starts for Aarogya Health Network were delayed because license verification and background verification were not completed before candidate presentation. The account team believed the candidates were start-ready, while the credentialing team still had unresolved evidence gaps. This mismatch created a customer-visible staffing miss, increased overtime coverage, and triggered an executive escalation from Kavya Raman.

The breach was avoidable. The main issue was not candidate availability. The main issue was process failure: credentialing checks were treated as a post-shortlist activity instead of a pre-shortlist readiness gate.

## 4. Customer Impact

The immediate impact was two delayed starts and 36 to 48 hours of additional overtime coverage depending on facility scheduling. The operational impact was larger than the shift count because Aarogya had to adjust nurse assignments at short notice. The relationship impact was significant because the customer believed Flow360 had represented the candidates as ready.

Commercial and renewal impact:

- Account sentiment moved from green to amber.
- Aarogya requested stricter credentialing visibility in future shortlists.
- Kavya Raman warned that repeated surprise blockers would trigger vendor review before renewal.
- Rohan Kulkarni requested stronger commercial justification for premium candidates.

## 5. Root Cause Analysis

### Primary Root Cause

Credentialing verification was requested after candidate presentation instead of before shortlist submission.

### Contributing Causes

1. The shortlist template did not require explicit labels for verified, conditional, blocked, or backup candidates.
2. Recruiter notes and compliance notes were kept in separate places.
3. The account manager treated candidate fit score as a proxy for readiness.
4. The team did not have an automated warning for starts inside five days with pending evidence.
5. Customer communication did not explain the risk until after the client selected the candidates.

### Five-Why Analysis

| Question | Answer |
| --- | --- |
| Why were starts delayed? | Selected candidates were not fully credentialed. |
| Why were they selected? | They were presented without clear conditional labels. |
| Why were labels missing? | The shortlist template did not require credentialing readiness fields. |
| Why was credentialing checked late? | The team followed a habit of validating after client interest rather than before shortlist. |
| Why was the risk not caught earlier? | There was no shared memory or pre-shortlist readiness gate across recruiter, compliance, and account teams. |

## 7. Preventive Actions

Future shortlists must separate candidates into verified, conditional, blocked, and backup categories. Any blocker must have an owner and due date. Conditional candidates may be shown only when the client hiring manager accepts the risk.

Flow360 should recommend escalation when it detects:

- Start date inside five days.
- License, BGV, immunization, or shift-consent blocker.
- Prior SLA incident for the same account.
- Renewal date inside 120 days.
- Executive sponsor dissatisfaction.

## 8. Lessons For Future Recommendations

The agent should not treat all candidate-fit scores equally. A high-fit candidate with pending license verification may be more risky than a lower-fit fully verified candidate. Recommendations should combine suitability, credentialing readiness, account history, commercial impact, and customer trust.

The next best action in a similar situation should be:

"Hold or mark the candidate conditional until credentialing clears. Escalate the blocker to Meera Nair and provide a verified backup option to Siddharth Menon before the shortlist deadline."
