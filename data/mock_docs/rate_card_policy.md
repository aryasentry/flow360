# Aarogya Health Network - Staffing Rate Card, Premium Exception, And Approval Policy

Document ID: AHN-RATE-CARD-2026-Q3
Version: 1.8
Effective Period: 2026-07-01 to 2026-09-30
Commercial Owner: Rohan Kulkarni, CFO
Account Owner: Flow360 Strategic Staffing Team

## 1. Purpose

This rate card policy defines standard bill-rate ranges, allowed variance, premium exceptions, approval requirements, and evidence expected before a rate exception can be presented to Aarogya Health Network. The document exists to prevent unstructured rate approvals during urgent staffing situations.

Aarogya has indicated that premium rates may be acceptable when vacancy cost, overtime risk, patient-care continuity risk, and backup availability are clearly explained. Premium rates without justification reduce trust and may affect renewal negotiations.

## 3. Premium Exception Criteria

Premium rate approval may be considered only when at least two of the following conditions are true:

- Start date is within five calendar days.
- Role supports ICU, emergency, hospital go-live, or other critical operation.
- Candidate is fully credentialed and available.
- Alternative candidates have lower fit, pending credentialing, or later availability.
- Vacancy creates measurable overtime cost.
- Prior SLA breach has increased renewal sensitivity.
- Customer executive sponsor requested urgent coverage.

Premium rate approval should not be used to compensate for poor planning, late sourcing, incomplete credentialing, or recruiter preference when acceptable candidates are available inside the standard band.

## 4. Required Premium Approval Brief

Any premium-rate request above the exception threshold must include a one-page approval brief with the following sections:

1. Candidate and role summary.
2. Requested bill rate and variance from standard card.
3. Start date and facility/city.
4. Credentialing status.
5. Vacancy cost estimate.
6. Overtime exposure estimate.
7. Patient-care or operational continuity risk.
8. Backup candidate availability and trade-off.
9. Recommended approval decision.

The brief must avoid raw resume details unless requested. It should focus on the business decision.

## 6. Aarogya-Specific Commercial Risk Notes

Aarogya is willing to accept a premium for verified ICU candidates when the alternative is overtime coverage or missed start dates. However, Rohan Kulkarni has explicitly requested cost-risk justification before approving premium candidates. Kavya Raman values speed but does not want surprise rate increases close to start date.

The safest recommendation is to separate candidates into:

- Standard-rate verified candidates
- Premium-rate verified candidates requiring approval
- Conditional candidates with unresolved credentialing blockers
- Backup candidates

## 7. Flow360 Recommendation Guidance

When rate variance appears in candidate data, Flow360 should combine rate card policy, candidate availability, credentialing status, open requisition urgency, and prior account risk before recommending approval. A recommendation should include:

- Whether to approve, hold, or request more data.
- Owner of the approval.
- Due date.
- Evidence sources.
- Business metric affected, such as overtime cost avoided or SLA risk reduced.

Example:

"Send premium-rate approval brief to Rohan Kulkarni by Friday 11 AM because Riya Menon is available before July 5, rate variance is 11 percent, and the account has active ICU overtime exposure. Attach vacancy-cost estimate, overtime comparison, and backup candidate table."
