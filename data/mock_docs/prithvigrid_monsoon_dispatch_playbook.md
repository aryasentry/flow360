# Playbook: Monsoon Dispatch And Critical Feeder Response

Document owner: Field Operations Excellence  
Safety reviewer: Ishita Rao  
Applies to: Transformer maintenance, hospital feeders, municipal feeders, high-risk monsoon events  
Version: 2026.06  
Classification: Field-service playbook, SLA and safety operations

## Purpose

This playbook defines how dispatch decisions should be made when weather, feeder criticality, technician certification, and customer communication must be balanced. It exists because PrithviGrid has recurring SLA risk during monsoon months.

## Dispatch Decision Inputs

The planner should consider:

- asset criticality
- downstream customer type
- open outage duration
- technician distance and availability
- safety refresher status
- lockout-tagout readiness
- weather and road conditions
- promised customer ETA
- previous SLA breach pattern

## Priority Rules

Hospital and municipal feeders have priority over routine transformer maintenance. However, safety compliance is non-negotiable. If the nearest crew has expired refresher status, dispatch must evaluate the next certified crew, even if travel time is longer.

Customer ETA updates must be sent every two hours for hospital feeders. The update must include assigned crew, safety approval status, ETA, risk to restoration, and next update time.

## Common Failure Modes

The most common failure modes are:

1. ETA promised before certification is checked.
2. Backup crew not evaluated until after preferred crew is delayed.
3. Weather risk treated as external excuse instead of operational signal.
4. Customer update missing safety status.
5. SLA breach RCA ignored in future dispatch recommendations.

## Recommended Flow360 Action Pattern

For critical feeder events, recommend:

1. Verify certified technician availability.
2. Compare primary and backup crew ETA.
3. Confirm lockout-tagout and PPE readiness.
4. Send customer ETA update with safety status.
5. Record final dispatch decision and reason in memory.
